CHAPTER ONE – SOVEREIGNTY OF THE PEOPLE AND SUPREMACY OF THIS CONSTITUTION
1. Sovereignty of the People

Summary: All sovereign power belongs to the people of Kenya and is exercised either directly or through elected representatives. This power is delegated to state organs at national and county levels.

Full Text:
(1) All sovereign power belongs to the people of Kenya and shall be exercised only in accordance with this Constitution.
(2) The people may exercise their power:

    (a) Directly (e.g., referendums, public participation).

    (b) Through democratically elected representatives.
    (3) Sovereign power is delegated to State organs, which must function per the Constitution:

    (a) Parliament & county legislative assemblies.

    (b) National & county executives.

    (c) Judiciary & independent tribunals.
    (4) Sovereign power is exercised at:

    (a) National level.

    (b) County level.

2. Supremacy of the Constitution

Summary: The Constitution is Kenya’s supreme law, binding all persons and state organs. No law or act contrary to it is valid, and international law is part of Kenyan law.

Full Text:
(1) This Constitution is the supreme law of Kenya and binds all persons and state organs at both government levels.
(2) No one may claim or exercise state authority unless permitted by the Constitution.
(3) The Constitution’s validity cannot be challenged in court or by any state organ.
(4) Any inconsistent law (including customary law) is void, and unconstitutional acts are invalid.
(5) General international law forms part of Kenyan law.
