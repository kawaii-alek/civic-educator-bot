CHAPTER FIVE – LAND AND ENVIRONMENT
PART 1 – LAND
60. Principles of Land Policy

Summary:
Guides equitable, sustainable land management through seven core principles implemented via national policy and legislation.

Full Text:
(1) Land shall be held, used and managed equitably, efficiently, productively and sustainably under these principles:
(a) Equitable access to land;
(b) Security of land rights;
(c) Sustainable management of land resources;
(d) Transparent and cost-effective administration;
(e) Conservation of ecologically sensitive areas;
(f) Elimination of gender discrimination in land matters;
(g) Community-based dispute resolution mechanisms.
(2) Implemented through:
(a) National land policy (developed/reviewed by national government);
(b) Legislation.
61. Classification of Land

Summary:
All land belongs collectively to Kenyans, categorized as public, community, or private.

Full Text:
(1) All land in Kenya belongs to the people collectively as:
(a) A nation;
(b) Communities;
(c) Individuals.
(2) Classified as:
(a) Public land;
(b) Community land;
(c) Private land.
62. Public Land

Summary:
Defines 14 categories of public land held in trust by national/county governments and administered by the National Land Commission.

Full Text:
(1) Public land includes:
(a) Former unalienated government land;
(b) Land used by state organs (except private leases);
(c) Land transferred to state via sale/reversion/surrender;
(d) Land with no identifiable owner/heir;
(e) Minerals and mineral oils;
(f) Government forests/game reserves/national parks;
(g) Roads and thoroughfares;
(h) Rivers/lakes/water bodies;
(i) Territorial waters, seabed, continental shelf;
(j) Land between high/low water marks;
(k) Unclassified land;
(l) Other land declared public by law.
(2) County governments hold in trust for residents:
(a) Categories (a), (c), (d), (e);
(b) Category (b) (excluding national organs).
(3) National government holds in trust:
Categories (f)-(l).
(4) Disposal/use only through parliamentary legislation.
63. Community Land

Summary:
Land held by ethnic/cultural communities with protections against arbitrary disposal.

Full Text:
(1) Vests in communities based on:
(a) Ethnicity;
(b) Culture;
(c) Shared interests.
(2) Includes:
(a) Registered group land;
(b) Legally transferred land;
(c) Declared community land;
(d) Community forests/grazing areas/ancestral lands.
(3) Unregistered land held in trust by county governments.
(4) Disposal/use only through specific legislation.
(5) Parliament to enact implementing laws.
64. Private Land

Summary:
Comprises freehold, leasehold, and other land declared private by law.

Full Text:
Private land consists of:
(a) Registered freehold land;
(b) Leasehold land;
(c) Other land declared private by Act of Parliament.
65. Landholding by Non-Citizens

Summary:
Restricts non-citizens to leasehold tenure (max 99 years) with safeguards against circumvention.

Full Text:
(1) Non-citizens may only hold leasehold (max 99 years).
(2) Any instrument granting >99 years automatically limited to 99.
(3) Corporate ownership:
(a) Only citizen-owned corporations qualify;
(b) Trusts must have 100% citizen beneficiaries.
(4) Parliament may enact further provisions.
66. Regulation of Land Use

Summary:
Allows state regulation for public interest and mandates community-benefit legislation.

Full Text:
(1) State may regulate land use for:
(a) Defence;
(b) Public safety/order/morality/health;
(c) Land use planning.
(2) Parliament to enact laws ensuring property investments benefit local communities.
67. National Land Commission

Summary:
Establishes an independent commission with comprehensive land management functions.

Full Text:
(1) Establishment of National Land Commission.
(2) Functions include:
(a) Manage public land;
(b) Recommend land policy;
(c) Advise on land registration;
(d) Research land/natural resource use;
(e) Investigate land injustices;
(f) Promote alternative dispute resolution;
(g) Assess land taxes;
(h) Oversee land use planning.
(3) May perform other legislated functions.
68. Legislation on Land

Summary:
Mandates comprehensive land law reforms and specific protections.

Full Text:
Parliament shall:
(a) Consolidate existing land laws;
(b) Align sectoral laws with Article 60 principles;
(c) Enact laws on:
(i) Minimum/maximum land holdings;
(ii) Land category conversion;
(iii) Matrimonial property protection;
(iv) Public land access/conservation;
(v) Review of past land grants;
(vi) Protection of dependants/spouses;
(vii) Other implementation matters.
PART 2 – ENVIRONMENT AND NATURAL RESOURCES
69. Environmental Obligations

Summary:
State must ensure sustainable environmental management with public participation.

Full Text:
(1) State shall:
(a) Ensure sustainable resource use;
(b) Maintain ≥10% tree cover;
(c) Protect biodiversity/indigenous knowledge;
(d) Encourage public participation;
(e) Establish EIA/audit/monitoring systems;
(f) Eliminate harmful activities;
(g) Use resources for public benefit.
(2) Every person has duty to cooperate in conservation.
70. Enforcement of Environmental Rights

Summary:
Provides judicial remedies for environmental violations without requiring proof of personal harm.

Full Text:
(1) Right to sue for environmental violations.
(2) Court may order:
(a) Cessation of harmful acts;
(b) State action to prevent harm;
(c) Compensation.
(3) No need to prove personal loss/injury.
71. Natural Resource Agreements

Summary:
Requires parliamentary ratification for resource exploitation contracts.

Full Text:
(1) Parliament must ratify:
(a) Resource exploitation agreements;
(b) Concessions granted post-Constitution.
(2) Parliament to legislate on ratification classes.
72. Environmental Legislation

Summary:
Mandates laws to implement environmental provisions.

Full Text:
Parliament shall enact legislation to give full effect to this Part.
