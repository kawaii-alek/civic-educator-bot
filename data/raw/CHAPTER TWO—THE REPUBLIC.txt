CHAPTER TWO – THE REPUBLIC
4. Declaration of the Republic

Summary: Kenya is a sovereign, multi-party democratic republic founded on national values and governance principles.
Full Text:
(1) Kenya is a sovereign Republic.
(2) It is a multi-party democratic state based on the national values in Article 10.
5. Territory of Kenya

Summary: Kenya’s territory includes its land, waters, and any additional areas defined by law.
Full Text:
Kenya consists of:

    The territory and waters existing at the Constitution’s effective date.

    Any additional territory/waters defined by Act of Parliament.

6. Devolution and Access to Services

Summary: Kenya is divided into counties; national and county governments must cooperate, and services must be accessible nationwide.
Full Text:
(1) Kenya’s territory is divided into counties (listed in the First Schedule).
(2) National and county governments are distinct but interdependent, operating through consultation and cooperation.
(3) National organs must ensure reasonable access to services across all regions, where feasible.
7. National, Official, and Other Languages

Summary: Kiswahili is the national language; English and Kiswahili are official. The state must protect linguistic diversity.
Full Text:
(1) Kiswahili is the national language.
(2) Kiswahili and English are the official languages.
(3) The state shall:

    (a) Protect Kenya’s language diversity.

    (b) Promote indigenous languages, Kenyan Sign Language, Braille, and accessible communication technologies.

8. State and Religion

Summary: Kenya has no state religion.
Full Text:
No state religion shall be established.
9. National Symbols and Days

Summary: Kenya’s symbols include the flag, anthem, coat of arms, and public seal. National days are public holidays.
Full Text:
(1) National symbols:

    (a) Flag

    (b) Anthem

    (c) Coat of arms

    (d) Public seal
    (2) Details are in the Second Schedule.
    (3) National days (public holidays):

    (a) Madaraka Day (1st June)

    (b) Mashujaa Day (20th October)

    (c) Jamhuri Day (12th December)
    (4) Parliament may establish additional public holidays.

10. National Values and Principles of Governance

Summary: State organs, officers, and all persons must uphold values like democracy, equity, and sustainable development.
Full Text:
(1) These values apply when:

    (a) Interpreting/applying the Constitution.

    (b) Enacting/interpreting laws.

    (c) Making public policies.
    (2) Values include:

    (a) Patriotism, unity, devolution, rule of law, democracy.

    (b) Human dignity, equity, social justice, equality, non-discrimination.

    (c) Good governance, integrity, transparency, accountability.

    (d) Sustainable development.

11. Culture

Summary: Culture is recognized as Kenya’s foundation. The state must promote cultural expression, science, and indigenous knowledge.
Full Text:
(1) Culture is Kenya’s foundation and civilization.
(2) The state shall:

    (a) Promote arts, literature, media, and cultural heritage.

    (b) Recognize science and indigenous technologies.

    (c) Protect intellectual property rights.
    (3) Parliament must enact laws to:

    (a) Compensate communities for use of their cultural heritage.

    (b) Protect indigenous seeds, plant varieties, and community rights.
