CHAPTER EIGHT – THE LEGISLATURE
PART 1 – ESTABLISHMENT AND ROLE OF PARLIAMENT
93. Establishment of Parliament

Summary:
Creates Kenya's bicameral Parliament consisting of the National Assembly and Senate.

Full Text:
(1) There is established a Parliament of Kenya consisting of:
(a) The National Assembly; and
(b) The Senate.
(2) Both Houses shall perform their functions in accordance with this Constitution.
94. Role of Parliament

Summary:
Vests legislative authority in Parliament and defines its constitutional responsibilities.

Full Text:
(1) National legislative authority is vested in Parliament.
(2) Parliament represents the people's will and diversity.
(3) Powers include:
(a) Constitutional amendments;
(b) County boundary changes.
(4) Shall protect the Constitution and democracy.
(5) Exclusive law-making power except where delegated.
(6) Delegated legislation must specify:
(a) Purpose/objectives;
(b) Authority limits;
(c) Nature/scope;
(d) Governing standards.
95. Role of the National Assembly

Summary:
Details the National Assembly's legislative, financial and oversight functions.

Full Text:
(1) Represents constituencies and special interests.
(2) Deliberates on public issues.
(3) Enacts legislation under Part 4.
(4) Financial roles:
(a) Revenue allocation between governments;
(b) National budget approval;
(c) Expenditure oversight.
(5) Oversight roles:
(a) Reviews President/DP/state officers;
(b) Monitors state organs.
(6) Approves:
(a) War declarations;
(b) Emergency extensions.
96. Role of the Senate

Summary:
Establishes the Senate as protector of county interests with specific legislative powers.

Full Text:
(1) Represents and protects county interests.
(2) Participates in county-related legislation (Articles 109-113).
(3) Determines county revenue allocation (Article 217).
(4) Participates in presidential removal (Article 145).
PART 2 – COMPOSITION AND MEMBERSHIP
97. National Assembly Membership

Summary:
Composition of 349 members with constituency and special representatives.

Full Text:
(1) Consists of:
(a) 290 constituency members;
(b) 47 county women reps;
(c) 12 special interest nominees (youth, disabled, workers);
(d) Speaker (ex officio).
(2) Does not restrict constituency candidacy.
98. Senate Membership

Summary:
68-member Senate with county, gender and special interest representation.

Full Text:
(1) Consists of:
(a) 47 county members;
(b) 16 women nominees;
(c) 2 youth reps (1M/1F);
(d) 2 disabled reps (1M/1F);
(e) Speaker (ex officio).
(2) Special reps elected under Article 90.
(3) Does not restrict county candidacy.
99. MP Qualifications

Summary:
Eligibility criteria and disqualifications for parliamentary candidates.

Full Text:
(1) Eligible if:
(a) Registered voter;
(b) Meets moral/educational standards;
(c) Party nominee or independent with:
(i) 1,000 supporters (NA);
(ii) 2,000 supporters (Senate).
(2) Disqualified if:
(a) State officer (except MP);
(b) Former IEBC member (5yrs);
(c) Citizen <10 years;
(d) MCA;
(e) Unsound mind;
(f) Undischarged bankrupt;
(g) Serving ≥6-month sentence;
(h) Violated leadership integrity (Chapter 6).
(3) Disqualification applies after appeals exhausted.
100. Marginalized Groups

Summary:
Mandates affirmative action for underrepresented groups.

Full Text:
Parliament shall enact laws to promote representation of:
(a) Women;
(b) Persons with disabilities;
(c) Youth;
(d) Ethnic minorities;
(e) Marginalized communities.
101. Election of MPs

Summary:
Election timetable and vacancy procedures.

Full Text:
(1) General elections every 5 years (2nd Tuesday August).
(2)-(5) Vacancy procedures:

    21-day notification;

    90-day by-elections (except 3mo pre-election);

    Party-list replacements within 21 days.

102. Term of Parliament

Summary:
5-year terms with emergency extensions.

Full Text:
(1) Term ends at next general election.
(2) Wartime extension (max 6mo at a time) by 2/3 majority.
(3) Total extension ≤12 months.
103. Vacation of Office

Summary:
Conditions for losing parliamentary seat.

Full Text:
(1) Vacates if:
(a) Dies;
(b) Absent ≥8 sittings unexcused;
(c) Removed under law;
(d) Resigns;
(e) Party member resigns/joins another party;
(f) Term ends;
(g) Disqualified under Art.99(2)(d)-(h).
(3) Parliament to legislate on party resignations.
104. Right of Recall

Summary:
Allows constituents to recall MPs before term ends.

Full Text:
(1) Constituents may recall MPs.
(2) Parliament to legislate procedures.
105. Membership Disputes

Summary:
High Court resolves election/seat disputes within 6 months.

Full Text:
(1) High Court determines:
(a) Election validity;
(b) Seat vacancies.
(2) Cases heard within 6 months.
(3) Parliament to enact procedures.
PART 3 – OFFICES OF PARLIAMENT
106. Speakers and Deputy Speakers

Summary:
Election and removal of presiding officers.

Full Text:
(1) Each House elects:
(a) Speaker (from non-MPs);
(b) Deputy Speaker (from members).
(2) Vacates if:
(a) New Parliament sits;
(b) Loses MP status;
(c) 2/3 removal vote;
(d) Resigns.
107. Presiding in Parliament

Summary:
Order of precedence for chairing sessions.

Full Text:
(1) House sittings chaired by:
(a) Speaker;
(b) Deputy Speaker;
(c) Elected member.
(2) Joint sittings chaired by NA Speaker assisted by Senate Speaker.
108. Party Leaders

Summary:
Recognition of majority/minority leaders.

Full Text:
(1) Majority and minority leaders recognized.
(2) Majority leader = largest party/coalition leader.
(3) Minority leader = second largest party/coalition leader.
(4) Order of precedence:
(a) NA Speaker;
(b) Majority leader;
(c) Minority leader.

PART 4 – PROCEDURES FOR ENACTING LEGISLATION
109. Exercise of Legislative Powers

Summary:
Outlines how Parliament exercises law-making authority through Bills.

Full Text:
(1) Parliament legislates through Bills passed and assented by the President.
(2) Bills may originate in the National Assembly.
(3) Non-county Bills:

    Considered only by NA;

    Passed per Article 122 and NA Standing Orders.
    (4) County Bills:

    May originate in NA or Senate;

    Passed per Articles 110–113, 122–123, and Standing Orders.
    (5) Money Bills:

    Introduced only in NA per Article 114.

110. Bills Concerning County Governments

Summary:
Defines county-related Bills and their classification.

Full Text:
(1) "County Bill" means:
(a) Affects county functions (Fourth Schedule);
(b) Relates to county elections;
(c) Impacts county finances (Chapter 12).
(2) Classified as:
(a) Special Bill if:
(i) County election-related; or
(ii) County Allocation of Revenue Bill (Article 218).
(b) Ordinary Bill otherwise.
(3) Speakers jointly resolve Bill classification disputes.
(4) Passed Bills referred to other House.
(5) Identically passed Bills sent to President within 7 days.
111. Special Bills Concerning Counties

Summary:
Special county Bills require 2/3 NA majority to amend/veto Senate version.

Full Text:
(1) Proceeds like ordinary county Bills.
(2) NA may amend/veto only by 2/3 majority.
(3) If NA fails to veto, Senate version sent to President.
112. Ordinary Bills Concerning Counties

Summary:
Mediation process for disagreed ordinary county Bills.

Full Text:
(1) If one House:
(a) Rejects Bill → Mediation Committee (Article 113);
(b) Amends Bill → Referred back to originating House.
(2) Originating House may:
(a) Accept amendments → Bill sent to President;
(b) Reject amendments → Mediation Committee.
113. Mediation Committees

Summary:
Process for reconciling disagreed county Bills.

Full Text:
(1) Speakers appoint equal-member committee.
(2) If committee agrees on version:

    Both Houses vote;

    If approved, sent to President within 7 days.
    (3) If committee fails or version rejected: Bill lapses.

114. Money Bills

Summary:
Restricts Money Bills to specified financial matters.

Full Text:
(1) Limited to matters in clause (3).
(2) NA proceeds per Finance Committee recommendation.
(3) "Money Bill" means legislation on:
(a) Taxes;
(b) Public fund charges;
(c) Appropriation/custody of public money;
(d) Loans;
(e) Incidental matters.
(4) Excludes county taxes/loans.
115. Presidential Assent and Referral

Summary:
President’s options for handling passed Bills.

Full Text:
(1) Within 14 days, President may:
(a) Assent; or
(b) Refer back with reservations.
(2) Parliament may:
(a) Amend per reservations; or
(b) Repass unchanged.
(3) If amended per reservations: Re-submitted for assent.
(4) If repassed unchanged/by 2/3 majority:

    Re-submitted within 7 days;

    President must assent within 7 days.
    (5) Automatic assent if President inacts.

116. Coming into Force of Laws

Summary:
Publication and commencement of Acts.

Full Text:
(1) Published in Gazette within 7 days of assent.
(2) Commences 14 days after publication unless specified otherwise.
(3) Acts benefiting MPs:

    Deferred until next general election.
    (4) Exception for public-interest benefits.

PART 5 – GENERAL PROCEDURES AND RULES
117. Powers, Privileges and Immunities

Summary:
Ensures parliamentary freedom and self-regulation.

Full Text:
(1) Freedom of speech/debate.
(2) Parliament may regulate:

    Powers/privileges;

    Immunities for members/committees.

118. Public Access and Participation

Summary:
Mandates open proceedings and public involvement.

Full Text:
(1) Parliament shall:
(a) Conduct open sittings;
(b) Facilitate public participation.
(2) Exclusion only in exceptional justified cases.
119. Right to Petition Parliament

Summary:
Citizens may petition for legislative action.

Full Text:
(1) Right to petition on any parliamentary matter.
(2) Parliament to establish procedures.
120. Official Languages

Summary:
Kiswahili, English, and Kenyan Sign Language recognized.

Full Text:
(1) Official languages: Kiswahili, English, KSL.
(2) Presidential-signed version prevails in conflicts.
121. Quorum

Summary:
Minimum members required for proceedings.

Full Text:
(a) NA: 50 members;
(b) Senate: 15 members.
122. Voting in Parliament

Summary:
Voting rules and restrictions.

Full Text:
(1) Majority of present/voting members decides.
(2) Speaker votes only to break ties.
(3) No voting on matters of pecuniary interest.
(4) Speaker excluded from membership count.
123. Decisions of the Senate

Summary:
Unique voting system for county-affected matters.

Full Text:
(1) County delegations (47) headed by elected senator.
(2) Speaker rules whether matter affects counties.
(3) Non-county matters: 1 senator = 1 vote.
(4) County matters:
(a) 1 vote per county delegation;
(b) Majority of delegations required.
124. Committees and Standing Orders

Summary:
Rules for committees and proceedings.

Full Text:
(1) Houses may establish committees and Standing Orders.
(2) Joint committees permitted.
(3) Proceedings valid despite irregularities.
(4) Public hearings for appointments.
125. Power to Call Evidence

Summary:
Parliamentary summons and evidence powers.

Full Text:
(1) Power to summon persons for evidence.
(2) Equivalent to High Court powers for:
(a) Witness attendance;
(b) Document production;
(c) Foreign evidence commissions.
PART 6 – MISCELLANEOUS
126. Location of Sittings

Summary:
Flexibility in parliamentary sittings.

Full Text:
(1) May sit anywhere in Kenya at appointed times.
(2) President gazettes first sitting (≤30 days post-election).
127. Parliamentary Service Commission

Summary:
Composition and functions of PSC.

Full Text:
(1) Established PSC.
(2) Composition:
(a) NA Speaker (Chair);
(b) Elected Vice-Chair;
(c) 7 MPs (4 govt, 3 opposition, gender-balanced);
(d) 2 public members (1M, 1F).
(3) Clerk of Senate = Secretary.
(4) Members vacate if:
(a) Parliament term ends;
(b) Cease being MPs;
(c) Appointed members revoked.
(5) Functions:
(a) Parliamentary services/facilities;
(b) Staff appointments;
(c) Budget preparation;
(d) Democracy promotion;
(e) Other legislated roles.
128. Clerks and Staff

Summary:
Appointment of parliamentary staff.

Full Text:
(1) Clerk for each House appointed by PSC with House approval.
(2) Clerks/staff form Parliamentary Service.
