CHAPTER THREE – CITIZENSHIP
12. Entitlements of Citizens

Summary: Every citizen enjoys constitutional rights and benefits, including a Kenyan passport, which can only be denied or revoked under parliamentary law.
Full Text:
(1) Citizens are entitled to:

    (a) Rights, privileges, and benefits of citizenship (within constitutional limits).

    (b) A Kenyan passport and state-issued identification documents.
    (2) Passports/documents may be denied, suspended, or confiscated only under an Act of Parliament compliant with Article 24 (limitation of rights).

13. Retention and Acquisition of Citizenship

Summary: Pre-existing citizenship is retained; new citizenship is acquired by birth or registration, and marriage does not affect status.
Full Text:
(1) Citizenship held before the Constitution’s effective date remains valid.
(2) Citizenship is acquired through:

    (a) Birth (Article 14).

    (b) Registration (Article 15).
    (3) Marriage or divorce does not revoke citizenship.

14. Citizenship by Birth

Summary: A person is Kenyan by birth if either parent is a citizen, including those born abroad. Found children under eight are presumed citizens.
Full Text:
(1) Citizenship by birth applies if either parent is Kenyan at birth (inside or outside Kenya).
(2) Retroactive for those born before the Constitution, if a parent is/was Kenyan.
(3) Parliament may limit citizenship for descendants of Kenyans born abroad.
(4) A child under eight found in Kenya with unknown parents is presumed a citizen.
(5) Kenyans who lost citizenship by acquiring another may reapply to regain it.
15. Citizenship by Registration

Summary: Foreign spouses, long-term residents, and adopted children may apply for citizenship.
Full Text:
(1) Spouses of citizens (married ≥7 years) may apply.
(2) Lawful residents (≥7 years) meeting parliamentary conditions may apply.
(3) Adopted children of citizens may apply.
(4) Parliament sets rules for granting citizenship to foreign nationals.
(5) Applies retroactively; pre/post-Constitution requirements count equally.
16. Dual Citizenship

Summary: Kenyan-born citizens retain citizenship even if they acquire another nationality.
Full Text:
A citizen by birth does not lose Kenyan citizenship by obtaining foreign citizenship.
17. Revocation of Citizenship

Summary: Citizenship may be revoked for fraud, treason, or serious crimes (for registered citizens) or if presumed birth citizenship is disproven.
Full Text:
(1) Registered citizens may lose citizenship if:

    (a) Obtained by fraud or false information.

    (b) Assisted enemies during war.

    (c) Convicted of a ≥3-year sentence within 5 years of registration.

    (d) Convicted of treason or offenses punishable by ≥7 years.
    (2) Presumed birth citizens (found children) may lose citizenship if:

    (a) Status was acquired by fraud.

    (b) Parentage/nationality reveals foreign citizenship.

    (c) Age is proven over eight years when found.

18. Legislation on Citizenship

Summary: Parliament must enact laws governing citizenship processes, residency, rights, and revocation.
Full Text:
Parliament shall legislate on:

    (a) Procedures for acquiring citizenship.

    (b) Entry and residence rules.

    (c) Permanent residency status.

    (d) Voluntary renunciation of citizenship.

    (e) Revocation procedures.

    (f) Duties and rights of citizens.

    (g) Implementation of this Chapter.
