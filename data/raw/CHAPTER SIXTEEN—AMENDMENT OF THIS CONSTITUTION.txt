CHAPTER SIXTEEN—AMENDMENT OF THIS CONSTITUTION
PART 1—GENERAL PROVISIONS

255. Amendment of this Constitution
Summary: Rules for amending the Constitution, including mandatory referendum clauses.

Full Text:
(1) Amendments requiring a referendum (per Article 256/257) relate to:
(a) Constitutional supremacy.
(b) Kenya’s territory.
(c) People’s sovereignty.
(d) National values/principles (Article 10(2)(a)–(d)).
(e) Bill of Rights.
(f) Presidential term.
(g) Independence of Judiciary/Commissions (Chapter 15).
(h) Parliament’s functions.
(i) Devolved government structure/principles.
(j) This amendment chapter.

(2) Referendum approval threshold:

    Turnout: ≥20% of voters in ≥½ of counties.

    Support: Simple majority of votes cast.

(3) Other amendments: Enacted via:

    Parliament alone (Article 256), or

    Popular initiative + Parliament (Article 257).

256. Amendment by Parliamentary Initiative
Summary: Process for Parliament-led amendments.

Full Text:
(1) Bill requirements:
(a) Introduced in either House.
(b) Limited to constitutional amendments + consequential legislation.
(c) 90-day gap between first and second readings.
(d) Passed by ≥2/3 majority in both Houses (second and third readings).

(2) Public participation: Parliament must publicize and facilitate debate.

(3) Post-passage:

    Speakers submit Bill + certification to the President.

(4) Presidential assent: Within 30 days (unless referendum required).

(5) If amendment falls under Article 255(1):
(a) President directs IEBC to hold a referendum within 90 days.
(b) Assent within 30 days after IEBC certifies referendum approval.

257. Amendment by Popular Initiative
Summary: Citizens’ power to propose amendments via petitions.

Full Text:
(1) Proposal: ≥1 million registered voters’ signatures.

(2) Form: General suggestion or draft Bill.

(3) If general suggestion, promoters must draft it into a Bill.

(4) IEBC verification: Confirms signature threshold.

(5) County assemblies: IEBC submits draft Bill to all counties for 3-month review.

(6) Approval: County speakers submit certified copies to Parliament if majority approve.

(7) Parliamentary introduction: Without delay if ≥½ counties approve.

(8) Passage: Simple majority in both Houses.

(9) Presidential assent: As per Article 256(4)–(5).

(10) Referendum triggered if:

    Either House rejects the Bill, or

    Amendment relates to Article 255(1) matters.

(11) Referendum rules follow Article 255(2).
