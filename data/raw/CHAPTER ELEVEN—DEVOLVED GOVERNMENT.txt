CHAPTER ELEVEN—DEVOLVED GOVERNMENT
PART 1—OBJECTS AND PRINCIPLES OF DEVOLVED GOVERNMENT

174. Objects of devolution
Summary: The objectives of devolving government powers to counties, including promoting democracy, unity, public participation, and equitable resource sharing.

Full Text:
The objects of devolution are—
(a) to promote democratic and accountable exercise of power;
(b) to foster national unity by recognizing diversity;
(c) to give powers of self-governance to the people and enhance their participation in decision-making;
(d) to recognize communities’ right to manage their own affairs and development;
(e) to protect and promote minority and marginalized groups;
(f) to ensure social and economic development with accessible services;
(g) to guarantee equitable sharing of national and local resources;
(h) to decentralize government functions from the capital;
(i) to strengthen checks and balances in governance.

175. Principles of devolved government
Summary: The foundational rules that county governments must follow, including democracy, financial sustainability, and gender balance.

Full Text:
County governments shall reflect the following principles—
(a) they shall operate democratically with separation of powers;
(b) they must have reliable revenue sources for effective service delivery;
(c) no more than two-thirds of their representative bodies shall be of the same gender.
PART 2—COUNTY GOVERNMENTS

176. County governments
Summary: Establishes the structure of county governments, consisting of an assembly and executive, with decentralized services.

Full Text:
(1) Each county shall have:
- A county assembly
- A county executive
(2) County governments shall decentralize their functions where efficient and practical.

177. Membership of county assembly
Summary: Describes the composition of county assemblies, including elected and nominated members.

Full Text:
(1) A county assembly consists of—
(a) members elected from each ward;
(b) special seat members to ensure no more than two-thirds are of one gender;
(c) representatives of marginalized groups (including persons with disabilities and youth);
(d) the Speaker (ex officio member).
(2) Special seat and marginalized group members shall be nominated by political parties based on election results.
(3) Special seat allocations are finalized after ward elections.
(4) County assemblies serve five-year terms.

178. Speaker of a county assembly
Summary: Rules for electing and removing the Speaker, who presides over assembly sessions.

Full Text:
(1) The Speaker is elected by the assembly from non-members.
(2) Assembly sessions are chaired by:
(a) the Speaker, or
(b) another member elected by the assembly if the Speaker is absent.
(3) Parliament shall enact laws on Speaker elections and removal.

179. County executive committees
Summary: Structure and functions of the county executive, led by the governor and deputy governor.

Full Text:
(1) Executive authority is exercised by the county executive committee.
(2) The committee consists of—
(a) the county governor and deputy governor;
(b) members appointed by the governor (approved by the assembly).
(3) Appointed members shall not exceed:
(a) one-third of the assembly if it has fewer than 30 members;
(b) ten members if the assembly has 30 or more.
(4) The governor is the chief executive; the deputy governor assists.
(5) The deputy governor acts as governor when the governor is absent.
(6) Executive members are accountable to the governor.
(7) If the governor’s office becomes vacant, appointed members cease office.

180. Election of county governor and deputy governor
Summary: Rules for electing governors and deputies, including qualifications and term limits.

Full Text:
(1) Governors are directly elected every five years.
(2) Candidates must qualify as county assembly members.
(3) A single candidate is declared elected unopposed.
(4) If multiple candidates run, the winner is determined by majority vote.
(5) Each governor candidate nominates a deputy governor.
(6) The deputy governor is declared elected alongside the governor.
(7) No person shall serve:
(a) as governor for more than two terms;
(b) as deputy governor for more than two terms.
(8) A governor who assumes office is deemed to have served a full term if more than 2.5 years remain in the term.

181. Removal of a county governor
Summary: Grounds and procedures for removing a governor from office.

Full Text:
(1) A governor may be removed for—
(a) violating the Constitution or other laws;
(b) serious criminal allegations under national/international law;
(c) abuse of office or gross misconduct;
(d) physical/mental incapacity.
(2) Parliament shall enact removal procedures.

182. Vacancy in the office of county governor
Summary: Rules for succession if the governor’s office becomes vacant.

Full Text:
(1) A vacancy occurs if the governor—
(a) dies;
(b) resigns in writing to the Speaker;
(c) becomes ineligible;
(d) is convicted of a serious offense;
(e) is removed from office.
(2) The deputy governor assumes office for the remainder of the term.
(3) If the deputy governor assumes office with over 2.5 years remaining, it counts as a full term.
(4) If both offices are vacant, the Speaker acts as governor.
(5) An election must be held within 60 days if the Speaker assumes office.
(6) The acting governor serves until a new governor is elected.

183. Functions of county executive committees
Summary: Responsibilities of county executives, including law implementation and reporting.

Full Text:
(1) The executive committee shall—
(a) implement county laws;
(b) implement national laws where required;
(c) manage county administration;
(d) perform other constitutional/national law functions.
(2) May propose legislation for the assembly.
(3) Must provide regular reports to the assembly.

184. Urban areas and cities
Summary: National laws govern urban areas, including classification and public participation.

Full Text:
(1) National legislation shall provide for—
(a) criteria for classifying urban areas;
(b) governance principles;
(c) resident participation in governance.
(2) May include mechanisms for different city categories.

185. Legislative authority of county assemblies
Summary: Powers of county assemblies to make laws and oversee executives.

Full Text:
(1) Legislative authority is vested in county assemblies.
(2) May make laws necessary for county functions.
(3) May oversee the county executive.
(4) May approve county development plans and policies.

PART 3—FUNCTIONS AND POWERS OF COUNTY GOVERNMENTS

186. Respective functions and powers of national and county governments
Summary: Defines how governmental functions are divided between national and county levels.

Full Text:
(1) The functions and powers are as specified in the Fourth Schedule.
(2) Where both levels have authority, it constitutes concurrent jurisdiction.
(3) Any function not assigned to counties remains with the national government.
(4) Parliament retains power to legislate on any matter for the Republic.

187. Transfer of functions and powers between levels of government
Summary: Rules for transferring governmental functions between national and county levels.

Full Text:
(1) Functions may be transferred if:
(a) The receiving government can perform them more effectively
(b) The transfer isn't prohibited by existing legislation
(2) When functions are transferred:
(a) Necessary resources must also be transferred
(b) Constitutional responsibility remains with the original level
PART 4—THE BOUNDARIES OF COUNTIES

188. Boundaries of counties
Summary: Process for altering county boundaries.

Full Text:
(1) Boundaries may only be changed by:
(a) Recommendation from an independent commission
(b) Approval by:
(i) 2/3 of National Assembly members
(ii) 2/3 of Senate county delegations
(2) Boundary changes must consider:
(a) Population density and trends
(b) Infrastructure
(c) Historical/cultural ties
(d) Administrative costs
(e) Community views
(f) Objects of devolution
(g) Geographical features
PART 5—RELATIONSHIPS BETWEEN GOVERNMENTS

189. Cooperation between national and county governments
Summary: Requirements for intergovernmental cooperation.

Full Text:
(1) Each level must:
(a) Respect the other level's integrity
(b) Assist and implement the other's legislation when appropriate
(c) Liaise and coordinate policies
(2) May establish joint committees/authorities
(3) Must attempt to resolve disputes reasonably
(4) National legislation shall provide alternative dispute resolution mechanisms

190. Support for county governments
Summary: National government's obligations to support counties.

Full Text:
(1) Parliament must ensure counties have adequate support
(2) Counties must maintain financial systems meeting national standards
(3) National government may intervene if:
(a) A county cannot perform its functions
(b) A county's financial system is non-compliant
(4) Intervention may include:
(a) Corrective measures
(b) Assuming responsibility for functions
(5) Intervention must:
(a) Give prior notice
(b) Be necessary
(c) Help restore county functions
(d) Allow Senate oversight

191. Conflict of laws
Summary: Rules for resolving conflicts between national and county laws.

Full Text:
(1) Applies to concurrent jurisdiction matters
(2) National law prevails if:
(a) It applies uniformly and:
(i) Cannot be effectively regulated by counties
(ii) Requires national uniformity
(iii) Is necessary for national security/unity
(b) Prevents unreasonable county actions
(3) County law prevails otherwise
(4) Courts should prefer interpretations avoiding conflict
(5) Conflicting provisions become inoperative but not invalid
PART 6—SUSPENSION OF COUNTY GOVERNMENTS

192. Suspension of a county government
Summary: Process for suspending county governments in emergencies.

Full Text:
(1) The President may suspend for:
(a) Internal conflict/war emergencies
(b) Other exceptional circumstances
(2) For non-emergency suspensions:
(a) Independent commission must investigate
(b) President must be satisfied
(c) Senate must authorize
(3) Parliament shall provide for function continuity during suspension
(4) Senate may terminate suspension
(5) Suspension cannot exceed 90 days
(6) Elections must be held after suspension ends
PART 7—GENERAL

193. Qualifications for election as member of county assembly
Summary: Requirements for county assembly candidates.

Full Text:
(1) Eligible if:
(a) Registered voter
(b) Meets educational/moral standards
(c) Nominated by party or as independent (with 500 supporters)
(2) Disqualified if:
(a) State/public officer (except MCA)
(b) Recent electoral commission member
(c) Citizen for <10 years
(d) Unsound mind
(e) Undischarged bankrupt
(f) Serving ≥6 month sentence
(g) Misused state office
(3) Disqualification applies after appeals exhausted

194. Vacation of office of member of county assembly
Summary: Circumstances when an MCA's seat becomes vacant.

Full Text:
(1) Vacancy occurs if member:
(a) Dies
(b) Misses 8 sittings without permission
(c) Is removed from office
(d) Resigns in writing
(e) Leaves party (for party members) or joins party (for independents)
(f) Term ends
(g) Becomes disqualified
(2) Parliament shall define party resignation circumstances

195. County assembly power to summon witnesses
Summary: Assembly's authority to compel testimony and documents.

Full Text:
(1) May summon anyone to give evidence
(2) Has same powers as High Court to:
(a) Enforce attendance and examine under oath
(b) Compel document production
(c) Examine witnesses abroad

196. Public participation and county assembly powers
Summary: Requirements for open governance and assembly privileges.

Full Text:
(1) Must:
(a) Conduct business openly
(b) Facilitate public participation
(2) Can only exclude public/media with Speaker's justification
(3) Parliament shall legislate on assembly privileges

197. County assembly gender balance and diversity
Summary: Rules for representation diversity in county governments.

Full Text:
(1) No more than 2/3 of assembly/executive of same gender
(2) Parliament shall legislate to:
(a) Reflect county diversity
(b) Protect minorities

198. County government during transition
Summary: Continuity of government during elections.

Full Text:
The outgoing executive remains competent until new officials assume office.

199. Publication of county legislation
Summary: Requirements for making county laws effective.

Full Text:
(1) County laws take effect upon Gazette publication
(2) Additional publication requirements may be prescribed

200. Legislation on Chapter
Summary: Parliament's duty to enact laws implementing devolution.

Full Text:
(1) Parliament shall pass necessary implementing legislation
(2) Including laws on:
(a) Capital/city governance
(b) Function transfers
(c) County official elections/removal
(d) Assembly procedures
(e) Suspension processes
