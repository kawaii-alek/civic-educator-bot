CHAPTER SIX—LEADERSHIP AND INTEGRITY
73. Responsibilities of Leadership

Summary: State officers hold authority as a public trust and must exercise it with integrity, accountability, and selfless service. Leadership principles include impartiality, honesty, and discipline.

Full Text:
(1) Authority assigned to a State officer:

    (a) Is a public trust exercised in a manner that:

        (i) Aligns with the Constitution’s purposes.

        (ii) Shows respect for the people.

        (iii) Upholds national honor and office dignity.

        (iv) Promotes public confidence in the office.

    (b) Requires service to the people, not power over them.

(2) Guiding principles of leadership and integrity:

    (a) Selection based on integrity, competence, or fair elections.

    (b) Objectivity and impartiality in decisions (no nepotism or corruption).

    (c) Selfless service, honesty, and declaration of conflicts of interest.

    (d) Accountability to the public.

    (e) Discipline and commitment in service.

74. Oath of Office of State Officers

Summary: State officers must take an oath before assuming office, as prescribed by law.

Full Text:
Before assuming, acting in, or performing State office functions, a person must take and subscribe to the oath or affirmation of office under the Third Schedule or an Act of Parliament.
75. Conduct of State Officers

Summary: State officers must avoid conflicts of interest, uphold public trust, and face dismissal for misconduct.

Full Text:
(1) A State officer must behave in a manner that avoids:

    (a) Conflicts between personal and public duties.

    (b) Compromising public interest for personal gain.

    (c) Demeaning the office.

(2) Violations (including breaches of Articles 76, 77, or 78(2) result in:

    (a) Disciplinary action.

    (b) Dismissal or removal from office.

(3) A person dismissed under this Article is disqualified from holding any other State office.
76. Financial Probity of State Officers

Summary: State officers cannot accept personal gifts, hold foreign bank accounts without approval, or take loans that compromise integrity.

Full Text:
(1) Gifts/donations to State officers on official occasions belong to the Republic unless exempted by law.
(2) A State officer shall not:

    (a) Maintain foreign bank accounts unless permitted by law.

    (b) Seek/accept personal loans or benefits that undermine integrity.

77. Restrictions on Activities of State Officers

Summary: Full-time State officers cannot engage in other employment, and retired officers face limits on post-service roles.

Full Text:
(1) Full-time State officers cannot take other gainful employment.
(2) Appointed State officers cannot hold political party offices.
(3) Retired State officers receiving pensions:

    (a) Cannot hold more than two paid positions in state-owned companies/organs.

    (b) Cannot receive other remuneration from public funds.

78. Citizenship and Leadership

Summary: State officers must be Kenyan citizens and cannot hold dual citizenship (with exceptions for judges and certain appointees).

Full Text:
(1) Only Kenyan citizens are eligible for State office.
(2) State officers and defense force members cannot hold dual citizenship.
(3) Exceptions:

    (a) Judges and commission members.

    (b) Persons who acquired foreign citizenship without choice (e.g., by automatic operation of law).

79. Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission

Summary: Parliament must establish an independent anti-corruption commission to enforce leadership standards.

Full Text:
Parliament shall enact legislation to establish an independent Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission with powers under Chapter Fifteen to enforce this Chapter.
80. Legislation on Leadership

Summary: Parliament must pass laws to enforce leadership rules, prescribe penalties, and extend principles to public officers.

Full Text:
Parliament shall enact laws to:

    (a) Establish procedures for administering this Chapter.

    (b) Prescribe penalties for violations.

    (c) Apply these principles (with modifications) to public officers.

    (d) Ensure enforcement of leadership and integrity standards.
