CHAPTER THIRTEEN—THE PUBLIC SERVICE
PART 1—VALUES AND PRINCIPLES OF PUBLIC SERVICE

232. Values and principles of public service
Summary: Establishes ethical standards and operational principles for all public service entities.

Full Text:
(1) The public service shall uphold:
(a) Professional ethics: High standards of integrity.
(b) Efficiency: Economic use of resources.
(c) Equitable service delivery: Responsive, impartial, and prompt.
(d) Public participation: Involvement in policy-making.
(e) Accountability: For administrative actions.
(f) Transparency: Timely and accurate public information.
(g) Merit-based appointments: Fair competition (subject to (h)–(i)).
(h) Diversity: Representation of all communities.
(i) Equal opportunities for:
- Men and women
- All ethnic groups
- Persons with disabilities

(2) Applies to:
(a) All state organs (national/county).
(b) State corporations.

(3) Parliament shall enact supporting legislation.
PART 2—THE PUBLIC SERVICE COMMISSION

233. The Public Service Commission (PSC)
Summary: Composition and eligibility rules for the PSC.

Full Text:
(1) The PSC is established.
(2) Members:

    Chairperson

    Vice-chairperson

    7 others
    (Appointed by the President with NA approval.)

(3) Disqualifications (5 years prior):
(a) MP/county assembly member or candidate.
(b) Political party governing body member.
(c) State office holder.
(d) Officeholder in political organizations sponsoring candidates.

(4) Restrictions in (3)(c)–(d) lapse after two general elections.

(5)–(6) Secretary:

    Chief executive, appointed by PSC for 5 years (renewable once).

234. Functions and powers of the PSC
Summary: Mandate over public service appointments, discipline, and reforms.

Full Text:
(2) The PSC shall:
(a) Establish/abolish offices and appoint/confirm public officers.
(b) Discipline and remove officers.
(c) Promote constitutional values (Articles 10 & 232).
(d) Investigate public service practices.
(e) Ensure efficiency and effectiveness.
(f) Develop human resources.
(g) Review conditions of service and codes of conduct.
(h) Report to the President/Parliament on compliance.
(i) Hear appeals from county public service.
(j) Perform other legislative functions.

(3) Exclusions:

    State offices (e.g., President, judges).

    Diplomatic roles.

    Offices under other commissions (e.g., Parliament, Judiciary, Teachers Service).

    County offices (except for appeals).

(4) Presidential staff appointments require the President’s consent.
(5) May delegate functions to members or public authorities.

235. Staffing of county governments
Summary: Counties manage their public service within national standards.

Full Text:
(1) Counties are responsible for:
(a) Creating/abolishing offices.
(b) Appointing/confirming staff.
(c) Disciplinary actions/removals.
(2) Excludes roles under the Teachers Service Commission.

236. Protection of public officers
Summary: Safeguards against unfair treatment of public servants.

Full Text:
No public officer shall be:
(a) Victimized/discriminated against for lawful performance of duties.
(b) Dismissed/demoted without due process.
PART 3—TEACHERS SERVICE COMMISSION

237. Teachers Service Commission (TSC)
Summary: Manages the teaching profession in public schools.

Full Text:
(1) The TSC is established.

(2) Functions:
(a) Register trained teachers.
(b) Recruit and employ teachers.
(c) Assign teachers to public schools.
(d) Promote/transfer teachers.
(e) Discipline teachers.
(f) Terminate employment.

(3) Advisory role:
(a) Review education/training standards.
(b) Assess teacher supply/demand.
(c) Advise the national government.
