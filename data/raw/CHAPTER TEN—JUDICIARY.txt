CHAPTER TEN—JUDICIARY
PART 1—JUDICIAL AUTHORITY AND LEGAL SYSTEM

159. Judicial authority

    Full Text:
    (1) Judicial authority derives from the people and vests in courts/tribunals established under this Constitution.
    (2) Courts/tribunals shall be guided by:
    (a) Equal justice for all
    (b) Timely justice
    (c) Promotion of alternative dispute resolution (ADR)
    (d) Avoidance of procedural technicalities
    (e) Protection of constitutional principles
    (3) Traditional dispute mechanisms shall not:
    (a) Violate Bill of Rights
    (b) Be repugnant to justice/morality
    (c) Contravene Constitution or written law

160. Independence of the Judiciary

    Full Text:
    (1) Judiciary subject only to Constitution/law
    (2) Judicial offices cannot be abolished while occupied
    (3) Judges' remuneration charged to Consolidated Fund
    (4) Judges' benefits protected from disadvantageous changes
    (5) Judicial immunity for acts done in good faith

161. Judicial offices and officers

    Full Text:
    (1) Comprises judges, magistrates, judicial officers and staff
    (2) Established offices:
    (a) Chief Justice (Head of Judiciary)
    (b) Deputy Chief Justice
    (c) Chief Registrar (administrator/accounting officer)
    (3) JSC may establish other registrar offices

162. System of courts

    Full Text:
    (1) Superior courts: Supreme Court, Court of Appeal, High Court
    (2) Parliament to establish specialized High Courts for:
    (a) Employment/labour relations
    (b) Environment/land disputes
    (4) Subordinate courts under Article 169

PART 2—SUPERIOR COURTS

163. Supreme Court

    Full Text:
    (1) Composition:
    (a) Chief Justice (President)
    (b) Deputy Chief Justice (Vice-President)
    (c) 5 other judges
    (2) Quorum: 5 judges
    (3) Jurisdiction:
    (a) Exclusive original jurisdiction over presidential election disputes
    (b) Appellate jurisdiction from Court of Appeal/other tribunals
    (4)-(5) Appeal conditions: constitutional matters/public importance
    (6) Advisory opinions on county government matters
    (7) Binding precedent on all other courts
    (8)-(9) Rule-making and operational legislation

164. Court of Appeal

    Full Text:
    (1) Minimum 12 judges; organization by Act of Parliament
    (2) President elected by judges
    (3) Appellate jurisdiction from High Court/other tribunals

165. High Court

    Full Text:
    (1) Composition/organization by Act of Parliament
    (2) Principal Judge elected by peers
    (3) Jurisdiction includes:
    (a) Unlimited original jurisdiction
    (b) Bill of Rights enforcement
    (c) Constitutional interpretation
    (d) Removal tribunal appeals (except Article 144)
    (4) Constitutional matters heard by uneven bench (≥3 judges)
    (5) Excluded jurisdictions (Supreme Court/specialized courts)
    (6)-(7) Supervisory powers over subordinate courts/quasi-judicial bodies

166. Appointment of judges

    Full Text:
    (1) Appointment process:
    (a) CJ/DCJ: JSC recommendation + NA approval
    (b) Other judges: JSC recommendation
    (2) General qualifications:
    (a) Law degree/advocate qualification
    (b) Relevant experience
    (c) High moral character
    (3)-(5) Experience requirements:
    - Supreme Court: 15 years
    - Court of Appeal: 10 years
    - High Court: 10 years

167. Tenure of office

    Full Text:
    (1) Retirement at 70 (optional from 65)
    (2) CJ serves max 10 years
    (3)-(4) Post-CJ service on Supreme Court
    (5) Resignation process

168. Removal from office

    Full Text:
    (1) Grounds: incapacity, misconduct, bankruptcy, incompetence
    (2)-(4) JSC initiates process; President appoints tribunal
    (5) Tribunal composition varies for CJ/other judges
    (6) Suspended judges receive half salary
    (7)-(10) Tribunal procedures and appeals

PART 3—SUBORDINATE COURTS

169. Subordinate courts

    Full Text:
    (1) Includes:
    (a) Magistrates' courts
    (b) Kadhis' courts
    (c) Courts Martial
    (d) Other tribunals
    (2) Parliament determines jurisdiction

170. Kadhis' Courts

    Full Text:
    (1) Minimum 3 Kadhis + Chief Kadhi
    (2) Qualifications: Muslim with Sharia law knowledge
    (3)-(5) Jurisdiction limited to Muslim personal law matters

PART 4—JUDICIAL SERVICE COMMISSION

171. Establishment of JSC

    Full Text:
    (1) Composition:
    (a) CJ (Chairperson)
    (b) Elected judges (Supreme, Appeal, High Court)
    (c) Elected magistrate
    (d) Attorney-General
    (e) Elected advocates (15+ years)
    (f) PSC nominee
    (g) Public representatives (President + NA approval)
    (3) Chief Registrar as Secretary
    (4) 5-year terms (one renewal)

172. Functions of JSC

    Full Text:
    (1) Responsibilities:
    (a) Judicial appointments
    (b) Conditions of service
    (c) Discipline of judicial officers
    (d) Judicial education
    (e) Justice administration advice
    (2) Guiding principles:
    (a) Transparent processes
    (b) Gender equality

173. Judiciary Fund

    Full Text:
    (1)-(2) Administered by Chief Registrar for judicial expenses
    (3)-(4) Budget approval process via National Assembly
    (5) Parliamentary regulation required
