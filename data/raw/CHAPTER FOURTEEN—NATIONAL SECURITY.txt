CHAPTER FOURTEEN—NATIONAL SECURITY
PART 1—NATIONAL SECURITY ORGANS

238. Principles of national security
Summary: Defines national security and its governing principles.

Full Text:
(1) National security protects Kenya against threats to:

    Territorial integrity and sovereignty

    People’s rights, freedoms, property, peace, and prosperity

(2) Guiding principles:
(a) Subject to constitutional and parliamentary authority.
(b) Compliance with law, human rights, and democracy.
(c) Respect for cultural diversity.
(d) Equitable recruitment reflecting Kenya’s diversity.

239. National security organs
Summary: Identifies Kenya’s security organs and their mandates.

Full Text:
(1) Organs:

    Kenya Defence Forces (KDF)

    National Intelligence Service (NIS)

    National Police Service (NPS)

(2) Primary objective: Promote national security per Article 238(2).

(3) Prohibited actions:

    Partisan or political bias.

    Promoting political party interests.

(4) No unauthorized paramilitary groups (except by law).

(5) Subordination to civilian authority.

(6) Parliament legislates on their functions and administration.

240. National Security Council (NSC)
Summary: Composition and roles of Kenya’s top security oversight body.

Full Text:
(1) The NSC is established.

(2) Members:

    President (Chair)

    Deputy President

    CSs for Defence, Foreign Affairs, Internal Security

    Attorney-General

    KDF Chief

    NIS Director-General

    NPS Inspector-General

(3) Functions:

    Supervises security organs.

    Integrates domestic/foreign/military policies.

    Assesses national security risks.

(6) Annual report to Parliament on security status.

(7) Deployments:

    Foreign KDF deployment (Parliament approval).

    Foreign forces in Kenya (Parliament approval).

244. NPS objectives
Summary: Standards for police conduct and operations.

Full Text:
The NPS shall:
(a) Uphold professionalism/discipline.
(b) Prevent corruption; ensure transparency.
(c) Respect human rights.
(d) Train staff to high standards.
(e) Foster public relationships.
