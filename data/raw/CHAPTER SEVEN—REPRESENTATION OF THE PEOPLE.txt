CHAPTER SEVEN – REPRESENTATION OF THE PEOPLE
PART 1 – ELECTORAL SYSTEM AND PROCESS
81. General Principles for the Electoral System

Summary:
Guarantees free, fair elections with gender balance, disability inclusion, and universal suffrage.

Full Text:
The electoral system shall comply with:
(a) Freedom to exercise political rights (Article 38);
(b) Not >2/3 of elective bodies of same gender;
(c) Fair representation of persons with disabilities;
(d) Universal suffrage based on fair representation;
(e) Free/fair elections through:
(i) Secret ballot;
(ii) Absence of violence/intimidation;
(iii) Independent management;
(iv) Transparency;
(v) Impartial/accountable administration.
82. Legislation on Elections

Summary:
Mandates parliamentary laws for electoral processes, including voter registration and special needs accommodations.

Full Text:
(1) Parliament shall enact laws on:
(a) Constituency delimitation by IEBC;
(b) Candidate nominations;
(c) Continuous voter registration;
(d) Election/referendum conduct;
(e) Progressive voting rights for diaspora.
(2) Voting systems must be:
(a) Simple;
(b) Transparent;
(c) Accommodate persons with disabilities/special needs.
83. Voter Registration

Summary:
Qualifies adult citizens for voting unless disqualified by mental incapacity or recent election offenses.

Full Text:
(1) Voter qualifications:
(a) Adult citizen;
(b) Not declared of unsound mind;
(c) No election offense conviction in past 5 years.
(2) Single registration center per voter.
(3) Administrative processes must facilitate voting rights.
84. Electoral Code of Conduct

Summary:
Requires candidates/parties to comply with IEBC’s conduct rules.

Full Text:
All candidates and parties shall comply with the IEBC-prescribed code of conduct.
85. Independent Candidates

Summary:
Allows independent candidacy with 3-month party non-affiliation and compliance with Articles 99/193.

Full Text:
Eligible if:
(a) Not a party member for ≥3 months pre-election;
(b) Meets qualifications under:
(i) Art. 99(1)(c)(i)/(ii) for National Assembly/Senate;
(ii) Art. 193(1)(c)(ii) for county assemblies.
86. Voting Procedures

Summary:
Mandates IEBC to ensure transparent, verifiable, and secure elections.

Full Text:
IEBC shall ensure:
(a) Simple/accurate/secure voting methods;
(b) Prompt counting/results at polling stations;
(c) Accurate collation by returning officers;
(d) Safeguards against malpractice.
87. Electoral Disputes

Summary:
Requires laws for timely dispute resolution (28-day petition window for non-presidential elections).

Full Text:
(1) Parliament to establish dispute mechanisms.
(2) Petitions (non-presidential): Filed within 28 days of results.
(3) Service via direct notice/newspaper advertisement.
PART 2 – INDEPENDENT ELECTORAL AND BOUNDARIES COMMISSION (IEBC)
88. IEBC Establishment and Functions

Summary:
Creates an independent commission to manage elections with strict eligibility criteria for members.

Full Text:
(1) Establishment of IEBC.
(2) Disqualifications for membership:
(a) Recent (5yrs) MP/county assembly/party office;
(b) Current state office.
(3) No dual public office for members.
(4) IEBC responsibilities include:
(a) Voter registration/roll revision;
(b) Constituency/ward delimitation;
(c) Candidate nomination regulation;
(d) Pre-results dispute resolution;
(e) Voter education;
(f) Campaign spending limits;
(g) Code of conduct enforcement.
89. Delimitation of Electoral Units

Summary:
Sets rules for constituency/ward boundaries (290 constituencies, 8–12yr reviews, ±40% population variance).

Full Text:
(1) 290 National Assembly constituencies.
(2) Boundary reviews every 8–12 years (completed ≥12mo pre-election).
(3) Periodic ward reviews.
(4) New boundaries ineffective if election is within 12 months.
(5)–(9) Population quotas with variances for:
(a) Geography/urban centers;
(b) Community ties;
(c) Sparsely populated areas (±40%);
(d) Other areas (±30%).
(10)–(11) High Court review within 30 days of gazettement (3-month determination).
(12) Defines "population quota."
90. Party List Seats Allocation

Summary:
Governs proportional representation for special seats (gender/ethnic diversity alternation).

Full Text:
(1) Applies to:
(a) National Assembly (Art. 97(1)(c));
(b) Senate (Art. 98(1)(b)–(d));
(c) County assemblies (Art. 177(1)(b)–(c)).
(2) IEBC ensures:
(a) Timely party candidate lists;
(b) Gender alternation in lists (except Senate);
(c) Regional/ethnic diversity (except county seats).
(3) Allocation proportional to seats won.
PART 3 – POLITICAL PARTIES
91. Political Party Requirements

Summary:
Mandates national character, internal democracy, and prohibits hate/religious-based parties.

Full Text:
(1) Parties must:
(a) Have national character;
(b) Elect leaders democratically;
(c) Promote national unity;
(d) Uhold good governance;
(e) Respect minority rights;
(f) Promote gender equality;
(g) Observe constitutional principles;
(h) Follow conduct codes.
(2) Prohibits:
(a) Religion/ethnicity-based parties;
(b) Violence/intimidation;
(c) Paramilitary groups;
(d) Corruption;
(e) Misuse of public resources.
92. Political Party Legislation

Summary:
Requires laws on media equity, party funding, and regulation.

Full Text:
Parliament shall enact laws on:
(a) Equitable media airtime;
(b) Fair campaign broadcasting;
(c) Party regulation/registration;
(d) Political Parties Fund;
(e) Party audits;
(f) Restrictions on public resources;
(g) Other management matters.
