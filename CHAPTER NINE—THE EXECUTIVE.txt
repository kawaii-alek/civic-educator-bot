CHAPTER NINE—THE EXECUTIVE
PART 1—PRINCIPLES AND STRUCTURE OF THE NATIONAL EXECUTIVE

129. Principles of executive authority

    Summary: Executive power originates from the people and must be exercised constitutionally for public service.

    Full Text:
    (1) Authority derives from Kenyans and follows constitutional provisions.
    (2) Must be exercised for citizens' well-being.

130. The National Executive

    Summary: Comprises President, Deputy President, and Cabinet, reflecting Kenya's diversity.

    Full Text:
    (1) Includes President, Deputy President, and Cabinet.
    (2) Composition ensures regional/ethnic representation.

PART 2—THE PRESIDENT AND DEPUTY PRESIDENT

131. Authority of the President

    Summary: Serves as Head of State/Government, Commander-in-Chief, and national unifier with constitutional duties.

    Full Text:
    (1) Roles: Leads government, chairs NSC, symbolizes unity.
    (2) Obligations: Uphold Constitution, sovereignty, rights, and diversity.
    (3) Barred from other state offices.

132. Functions of the President

    Summary: Includes parliamentary addresses, appointments, governance, and emergency powers.

    Full Text:
    (1) Annual reports to Parliament/nation on governance.
    (2) Appoints Cabinet, AG, diplomats (with NA approval).
    (3) Leads Cabinet, assigns ministerial roles.
    (4) Additional powers: honors, emergencies (with Parliament).
    (5) Ensures international commitments are met.

133. Power of mercy

    Summary: May pardon offenders based on Advisory Committee recommendations.

    Full Text:
    (1) Can pardon, delay, or reduce punishments.
    (2) Committee: AG, Correctional Services CS + 5+ members.
    (3) Parliament regulates Committee procedures.
    (4) Committee may consult victims.

134. Exercise of presidential powers during temporary incumbency

    Summary: Acting President restricted in appointments/pardons during elections/incapacity.

    Full Text:
    (1) Interim leaders cannot make key appointments.
    (2) Restrictions cover judicial/state posts, mercy, honors.

135. Decisions of the President

    Summary: Official decisions require written documentation with seal/signature.

    Full Text: Must be written and bear presidential seal/signature.

136. Election of the President

    Summary: Nationally elected every 5 years alongside parliamentary polls.

    Full Text:
    (1) Elected per constitutional/legal guidelines.
    (2) Timing: Aligned with general elections or vacancy (per 146).

137. Qualifications and disqualifications for election as President

    Full Text:
    (1) To qualify for presidential nomination, a person must:
    (a) Be a citizen by birth
    (b) Be qualified to stand for election as Member of Parliament
    (c) Be nominated by a political party or as an independent candidate
    (d) Be nominated by at least two thousand voters from each of a majority of counties
    (2) A person is disqualified if they:
    (a) Owe allegiance to a foreign state
    (b) Are public officers (except President, Deputy President or MP)

138. Procedure at presidential election

    Full Text:
    (1) Only one candidate nominated: declared elected
    (2) Multiple candidates: election held in each constituency
    (3) Election conducted by:
    (a) All registered voters
    (b) Secret ballot as prescribed by law
    (c) IEBC to tally, verify and declare results
    (4) Winning candidate must receive:
    (a) More than half of all votes cast
    (b) At least 25% of votes in more than half of counties
    (5)-(9) Runoff election procedures if no winner
    (10) IEBC to declare results within 7 days

139. Death before assuming office

    Full Text:
    (1) If President-elect dies:
    (a) Deputy President-elect becomes Acting President
    (b) Fresh election within 60 days
    (2) If Deputy President-elect dies: office declared vacant
    (3) If both die:
    (a) Speaker acts as President
    (b) Fresh election within 60 days

140. Questions as to validity of presidential election

    Full Text:
    (1) Petition to Supreme Court within 7 days of results
    (2) Court to hear and determine within 14 days
    (3) If election invalidated: fresh election within 60 days

141. Assumption of office of President

    Full Text:
    (1) Sworn in publicly before Chief Justice
    (2) Timing:
    (a) 14 days after declaration if no petition
    (b) 7 days after court decision if petition filed
    (3) Takes oaths as per Third Schedule
    (4) Parliament to legislate swearing-in procedure

142. Term of office of President

    Full Text:
    (1) Term begins at swearing-in, ends when successor sworn in
    (2) Maximum two terms

143. Protection from legal proceedings

    Full Text:
    (1) No criminal proceedings against sitting President
    (2) No civil proceedings for official acts
    (3) Presidential tenure not counted in statutory limitation periods
    (4) Exception for crimes under international treaties

144. Removal of President on grounds of incapacity

    Full Text:
    (1) Motion by at least 25% of NA members
    (2) If majority supports: Speaker informs Chief Justice
    (3) Tribunal appointed within 7 days (3 doctors, 1 lawyer, 1 nominee)
    (6) Tribunal reports within 14 days
    (9)-(10) NA votes to ratify removal

145. Removal of President by impeachment

    Full Text:
    (1) Motion by at least 1/3 of NA members
    (2) Requires 2/3 NA support to proceed
    (3) Senate hears charges, may appoint committee
    (6)-(7) 2/3 Senate vote required for removal

146. Vacancy in the office of President

    Full Text:
    (1) Vacancy occurs through: death, resignation, removal
    (2) Succession:
    (a) Deputy President assumes office
    (b) If no DP: Speaker acts, election within 60 days
    (3) Successor serves remainder of term

147. Functions of the Deputy President

    Full Text:
    (1) Principal assistant to President
    (2) Performs functions assigned by President
    (3) Acts as President during absence/incapacity
    (4) Cannot hold other state office

148. Election and swearing in of Deputy President

    Full Text:
    (1) Presidential candidate nominates DP candidate
    (3) IEBC declares winning DP candidate
    (4)-(5) Sworn in before Chief Justice
    (6) Term aligns with President's
    (8) Maximum two terms

149. Vacancy in the office of Deputy President

    Full Text:
    (1) President nominates replacement within 14 days
    (2) NA must approve within 60 days

150. Removal of Deputy President

    Full Text:
    (1) Grounds: incapacity, violation, crimes, misconduct
    (2) Follows same process as presidential removal

151. Remuneration and benefits of President and Deputy President

    Full Text:
    (1) Paid from Consolidated Fund
    (2) Cannot be reduced during office
    (3) Retirement benefits protected

PART 3—THE CABINET

152. Cabinet

    Full Text:
    (1) Composition: President, DP, AG, 14-22 CSs
    (2) Appointment: President nominates, NA approves
    (5)-(10) Dismissal process through NA resolution

153. Decisions, responsibility and accountability of the Cabinet

    Full Text:
    (1) Decisions must be in writing
    (2) Collective responsibility to President
    (3) Must appear before parliamentary committees
