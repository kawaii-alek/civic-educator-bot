CHAPTER FIFTEEN—COMMISSIONS AND INDEPENDENT OFFICES
PART 1—GENERAL PROVISIONS

248. Application of Chapter
Summary: Lists commissions and independent offices covered by this chapter.

Full Text:
(1) Applies to all commissions and independent offices listed below, unless the Constitution provides otherwise.

(2) Commissions:

    Kenya National Human Rights and Equality Commission

    National Land Commission

    Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC)

    Parliamentary Service Commission

    Judicial Service Commission

    Commission on Revenue Allocation

    Public Service Commission

    Salaries and Remuneration Commission

    Teachers Service Commission

    National Police Service Commission

(3) Independent Offices:

    Auditor-General

    Controller of Budget

249. Objects, Authority, and Funding
Summary: Mandate and independence of commissions/offices.

Full Text:
(1) Objectives:
(a) Protect people’s sovereignty.
(b) Ensure state organs uphold democratic values.
(c) Promote constitutionalism.

(2) Independence:
(a) Subject only to the Constitution and law.
(b) Free from control by any person/authority.

(3) Funding:

    Parliament must allocate adequate funds.

    Each entity’s budget is a separate vote.

250. Composition, Appointment, and Terms
Summary: Rules for appointing commissioners and officeholders.

Full Text:
(1) Commissions have 3–9 members.

(2) Appointment Process:
(a) Identified/recommended per national legislation.
(b) Approved by the National Assembly.
(c) Appointed by the President.

(3) Qualifications: As prescribed by Constitution/law.

(4) Diversity: Appointments must reflect regional/ethnic diversity.

(5) Members may serve part-time.

(6) Term Limits:
(a) Single 6-year term (no reappointment).
(b) No other paid employment (unless ex officio/part-time).

(7)–(8) Remuneration:

    Charged to Consolidated Fund.

    Cannot be reduced disadvantageously.

(9) Legal Protection: No liability for acts done in good faith.

(10)–(11) Leadership:

    Members elect a vice-chairperson (not same gender as chair).

(12) Secretary:

    Appointed by the commission.

    Serves as CEO.

251. Removal from Office
Summary: Grounds and process for removal.

Full Text:
(1) Grounds:
(a) Violation of Constitution/law (including Chapter Six).
(b) Gross misconduct.
(c) Physical/mental incapacity.
(d) Incompetence.
(e) Bankruptcy.

(2)–(3) Process:

    Petition to NA → Investigation → Tribunal if grounds exist.

(4)–(5) Tribunal:

    Chair: Superior court judge.

    2+ qualified High Court judges.

    1 specialist member.

(6) Tribunal’s recommendation is binding on the President.

(7) Suspended members receive half salary.

252. General Functions and Powers
Summary: Common powers of commissions/offices.

Full Text:
(1) Powers:
(a) Investigate complaints or initiate probes.
(b) Conciliate, mediate, negotiate.
(c) Recruit own staff.
(d) Perform additional legislative functions.

(2) Who Can Complain: Anyone eligible to sue under Article 22(1)–(2).

(3) Summons Power:

    Kenya Human Rights Commission

    Judicial Service Commission

    National Land Commission

    Auditor-General

253. Incorporation
Summary: Legal status of commissions/offices.

Full Text:
Each entity:
(a) Is a body corporate (perpetual existence, seal).
(b) Can sue/be sued in its name.

254. Reporting
Summary: Transparency and accountability requirements.

Full Text:
(1) Annual reports to President and Parliament.

(2) Special reports on demand by President, NA, or Senate.

(3) All reports must be published publicly.
