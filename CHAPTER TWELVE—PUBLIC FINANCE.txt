CHAPTER TWELVE—PUBLIC FINANCE
PART 1—PRINCIPLES AND FRAMEWORK OF PUBLIC FINANCE

201. Principles of public finance
Summary: Establishes the guiding principles for managing public finances, emphasizing transparency, equity, and responsible fiscal management.

Full Text:
The following principles shall guide all aspects of public finance in Kenya—
(a) Openness and accountability: Ensure public participation in financial matters.
(b) Equitable society:

    Fair sharing of tax burdens.

    Equitable revenue sharing between national and county governments.

    Expenditure that promotes equitable development, including marginalized groups.
    (c) Intergenerational equity: Fair sharing of resource burdens/benefits between present and future generations.
    (d) Prudent use of public funds: Responsible spending.
    (e) Clear fiscal reporting: Transparent financial management.

202. Equitable sharing of national revenue
Summary: Mandates fair distribution of nationally raised revenue between national and county governments.

Full Text:
(1) Revenue raised nationally shall be shared equitably between national and county governments.
(2) Counties may receive additional conditional or unconditional allocations from the national share.

203. Equitable share and other financial laws
Summary: Criteria for determining revenue shares and financial legislation affecting counties.

Full Text:
(1) The following factors must be considered:
(a) National interest.
(b) Public debt obligations.
(c) Objective needs of the national government.
(d) Counties’ capacity to perform functions.
(e) Fiscal efficiency of counties.
(f) Developmental needs of counties.
(g) Economic disparities and affirmative action.
(h) Incentives for revenue optimization.
(i) Predictability of allocations.
(j) Flexibility for emergencies.
(2) Counties’ share shall not be less than 15% of national revenue.
(3) Calculations based on the most recent audited accounts approved by the National Assembly.

204. Equalisation Fund
Summary: Establishes a fund to uplift marginalized areas by providing basic services.

Full Text:
(1) 0.5% of national revenue is allocated annually to the Equalisation Fund.
(2) Funds are used for:

    Water, roads, health, and electricity in marginalized areas.

    Aimed at raising service levels to national standards.
    (3) Usage conditions:

    Approved by Parliament via Appropriation Bill.

    May be disbursed directly or as conditional grants.
    (4) Commission on Revenue Allocation (CRA) must be consulted.
    (5) Unexpended funds roll over to subsequent years.
    (6) Sunset clause: Fund lapses after 20 years (extendable by Parliament).
    (9) Withdrawals require Controller of Budget approval.

205. Consultation on financial legislation affecting counties
Summary: Requires CRA input on bills impacting county finances.

Full Text:
(1) CRA reviews and recommends changes to bills on revenue sharing or county finances.
(2) Parliament must table and consider CRA’s recommendations before voting.
PART 2—OTHER PUBLIC FUNDS

206. Consolidated Fund and other public funds
Summary: Central fund for national revenue, with strict withdrawal rules.

Full Text:
(1) All national revenue is paid into the Consolidated Fund unless:

    Excluded by law for specific public funds.

    Retained by state organs for operational expenses (per law).
    (2) Withdrawals only allowed via:

    Parliamentary appropriation.

    Articles 222 (emergency funds) or 223 (supplementary funds).

    Constitutional/statutory charges.
    (4) Controller of Budget must approve withdrawals.

207. County Revenue Funds
Summary: Each county has a dedicated revenue fund.

Full Text:
(1) All county revenue is paid into its Revenue Fund (unless excluded by law).
(2) Withdrawals permitted only for:

    Lawful charges.

    County appropriation laws.
    (3) Controller of Budget must approve withdrawals.
    (4) Parliament may legislate on county fund management.

208. Contingencies Fund
Summary: Emergency fund for urgent, unforeseen expenditures.

Full Text:
(1) Established and managed per Act of Parliament.
(2) Cabinet Secretary for Finance may authorize advances if:

    Needs are urgent/unforeseen.

    No other funding exists.

PART 3—REVENUE-RAISING POWERS AND THE PUBLIC DEBT

209. Power to impose taxes and charges
Summary: Defines which levels of government can impose specific taxes and charges.

Full Text:
(1) Exclusive national taxes:

    Income tax

    Value-added tax (VAT)

    Customs duties

    Excise tax
    (2) Parliament may authorize other national taxes (except county taxes in (3)).
    (3) County taxes:

    Property rates

    Entertainment taxes

    Others authorized by Parliament
    (4) Both levels may charge fees for services.
    (5) County taxes must not harm national economic policies or cross-county trade.

210. Imposition of tax
Summary: Rules for imposing/waiving taxes, with transparency requirements.

Full Text:
(1) Taxes/fees can only be imposed, waived, or changed through legislation.
(2) If waived:

    Public records must show the waiver and reason.

    Reported to Auditor-General.
    (3) No tax exemptions for State officers based on office or work nature.

211. Borrowing by national government
Summary: Parliament regulates national debt with transparency rules.

Full Text:
(1) Parliament legislates on:

    Borrowing terms

    Reporting requirements
    (2) Within 7 days of request, Finance CS must disclose to Parliament:

    Total debt (principal + interest)

    Loan purpose

    Repayment plans

    Current repayment progress

212. Borrowing by counties
Summary: Counties may borrow only with national guarantees and county assembly approval.

Full Text:
A county government may borrow only if:
(a) The national government guarantees the loan;
(b) Its county assembly approves.

213. Loan guarantees by national government
Summary: Rules for national guarantees of county/national loans.

Full Text:
(1) Parliament prescribes guarantee terms.
(2) Annual report on guarantees must be published within 2 months after each financial year.

214. Public debt
Summary: Public debt is charged to the Consolidated Fund unless reallocated by law.

Full Text:
(1) Public debt includes all loans/guarantees by the national government.
(2) Parliament may allocate debt to other public funds.
PART 4—REVENUE ALLOCATION

215. Commission on Revenue Allocation (CRA)
Summary: Independent body to recommend fair revenue sharing.

Full Text:
(1) CRA is established.
(2) Members:

    Chairperson (President nominates, NA approves)

    2 NA party nominees

    5 Senate party nominees

    Finance PS (non-voting)
    (3) Members must have financial/economic expertise.

216. Functions of CRA
Summary: Advises on revenue sharing and fiscal management.

Full Text:
(1) Recommends equitable sharing of national revenue between:

    National/county governments

    Among counties
    (2) Advises on county financial management.
    (3) Considers:

    Article 203(1) criteria

    Enhancing revenue sources

    Fiscal responsibility
    (4) Publishes policy to identify marginalized areas (for Equalisation Fund).
    (5) Submits recommendations to Parliament, executives, and counties.

217. Division of revenue
Summary: Senate determines county revenue shares every 5 years.

Full Text:
(1) Senate resolves the basis for allocating county shares.
(2) Must:

    Apply Article 203(1) criteria

    Consider CRA recommendations

    Consult governors, Finance CS, and public
    (3)–(9) Process for NA approval/mediation if Senate/NA disagree.

218. Annual Division and Allocation of Revenue Bills
Summary: Bills to formalize revenue sharing, with transparency requirements.

Full Text:
(1) Two bills introduced annually:

    Division of Revenue Bill: National/county shares.

    County Allocation of Revenue Bill: County shares.
    (2) Bills must include:

    Revenue allocation explanations

    Evaluation against Article 203(1) criteria

    Reasons for deviating from CRA recommendations

219. Transfer of equitable share
Summary: County funds must be transferred promptly unless halted under Article 225.

Full Text:
County shares are transferred without delay/deduction, unless stopped for financial mismanagement.
PART 5—BUDGETS AND SPENDING

220. Form and content of budgets
Summary: Budgets must detail revenue, expenditure, and borrowing plans.

Full Text:
(1) Budgets must show:

    Recurrent vs. development expenditure

    Deficit financing plans

    Proposed borrowing
    (2) National legislation prescribes county budget formats and timelines.

221. National budget process
Summary: Annual budget submission, scrutiny, and approval.

Full Text:
(1) Finance CS submits estimates to NA 2+ months before year-end.
(2) Includes Equalisation Fund expenditures.
(3)–(5) NA committee reviews with public input.
(6) Appropriation Bill authorizes withdrawals (excludes constitutionally charged expenditures).

222. Interim spending
Summary: Allows limited spending if budget is delayed.

Full Text:
(1) If Appropriation Act is delayed, NA may authorize withdrawals up to 50% of tabled estimates.
(2) Funds are for essential services pending budget approval.

223. Supplementary budgets
Summary: Rules for unplanned expenditures.

Full Text:
(1) Additional spending allowed if:

    Appropriated funds are insufficient, or

    Contingencies Fund used.
    (2) Parliament must approve within 2 months (or 2 weeks if not sitting).
    (5) Caps supplementary spending at 10% of annual budget (unless Parliament approves more).

224. County appropriation Bills
Summary: Counties prepare budgets based on Division of Revenue Bill.

Full Text:
Each county adopts its budget per national legislation, using allocated revenue.
PART 6—CONTROL OF PUBLIC MONEY

225. Financial control
Summary: Mechanisms to enforce fiscal discipline, including fund freezes.

Full Text:
(1) National Treasury established by law.
(2) Parliament legislates expenditure controls.
(3) Finance CS may stop transfers to entities for serious breaches (max 50% of county funds).
(4)–(7) Freezes lapse unless approved by Parliament within 30 days (renewable for 60 days).

226. Accounts and audit
Summary: Transparency rules for public entities.

Full Text:
(1) Law requires:

    Financial record-keeping

    Audits

    Designated accounting officers
    (2) Accounting officers answer to NA/county assemblies.
    (3) Auditor-General audits all governments/state organs (except own office: audited by NA-appointed accountant).
    (5) Officials liable for unlawful spending.

227. Public procurement
Summary: Fair, competitive, and transparent procurement rules.

Full Text:
(1) Contracts must be:

    Fair, equitable, transparent, competitive, cost-effective.
    (2) Legislation may provide for:

    Preference categories (e.g., disadvantaged groups)

    Sanctions for poor performance/tax defaults/corruption

228. Controller of Budget
Summary: Oversees budget implementation.

Full Text:
(1) Appointed by President (NA approval).
(2) Requires 10+ years’ finance/audit experience.
(3) 8-year term (no reappointment).
(4)–(5) Authorizes withdrawals only if lawful.
(6) Submits quarterly budget reports to Parliament.

229. Auditor-General
Summary: Audits all public entities annually.

Full Text:
(1) Appointed like Controller of Budget.
(4) Audits:

    National/county governments

    Courts, commissions, Parliament, political parties, public debt.
    (6) Reports on lawful/effective use of funds.
    (7)–(8) Reports debated by Parliament/assemblies within 3 months.

230. Salaries and Remuneration Commission
Summary: Sets salaries for state/public officers.

Full Text:
(1) Members include nominees from:

    Parliamentary/Judicial/Public Service Commissions

    Trade unions, employers, professionals

    Finance CS, Attorney-General
    (4) Functions:

    Set state officers’ pay

    Advise on other public officers’ pay
    (5) Considers:

    Fiscal sustainability

    Talent retention

    Performance, fairness

231. Central Bank of Kenya
Summary: Independent monetary authority.

Full Text:
(1) Mandate:

    Monetary policy

    Price stability

    Currency issuance
    (3) Operates free from external control.
    (4) Currency may depict Kenya (not individuals).
