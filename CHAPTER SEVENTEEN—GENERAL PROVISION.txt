CHAPTER SEVENTEEN—GENERAL PROVISIONS
PART 1—CONSTITUTIONAL ENFORCEMENT AND INTERPRETATION

258. Enforcement of this Constitution
Summary: Rights and mechanisms for upholding constitutional compliance.

Full Text:
(1) Any person may sue to challenge:

    A constitutional violation, or

    An imminent threat of violation.

(2) Standing to sue extends to:
(a) Representatives of incapacitated persons.
(b) Class/group advocates.
(c) Public interest litigants.
(d) Associations acting for members.

259. Construing this Constitution
Summary: Principles for interpreting the Constitution.

Full Text:
(1) Interpretation must:
(a) Promote constitutional purposes/values.
(b) Advance rule of law and human rights.
(c) Allow legal evolution.
(d) Support good governance.

(2) Language: English version prevails in conflicts.

(3) Doctrine of "law is always speaking":
(a) Powers vest in current officeholders.
(b) References to offices include acting officers.
(c) Formal name changes apply contextually.
(d) Defunct entities refer to successors.

(4) Definitions:

    Defined terms include grammatical variations.

    "Includes" = non-exhaustive examples.

(5)–(9) Time calculations:

    Exclude start day; include end day.

    Months/years end on corresponding date (or month’s end).

    ≤6-day periods exclude Sundays/holidays.

    Extend deadlines falling on holidays.

(10) Reappointment: Vacated offices may be refilled if qualified.

(11) Conditional powers: Require stipulated advice/consultation unless exempted.
